CHAPTER RESEARCH EDITION

The argument, evidence and visual route

This chapter is expanded through the research plan below. It is written as a reading route, not a placeholder checklist.

  • Narrative angle: unstructured play and unsupervised outdoor time as an unequally distributed resource — space, safety, and time all gate it.
  • Historical context: urbanization's shrinking of public/informal play space; the RTE Act's playground infrastructure norms and their inconsistent enforcement.
  • Mechanism: three constraints jointly determine play access: physical space (urban density, safe outdoor area), time (chores/work competing for hours), and safety (parental risk perception, especially for girls).
  • India context: contrast government-school infrastructure norms (RTE mandated) with actual compliance data.
  • Counterarguments: organized sports/play programs (Khelo India, school sports initiatives) have expanded; not all urban children lack access.
  • Limitations: "play access" is rarely measured directly in national surveys; the chapter should be honest that this is argued more from infrastructure/time-use proxies than a direct dataset.
  • Data [VERIFY]: UDISE+ school playground-availability indicator; Time Use Survey child leisure-time figures if disaggregated by age.
  • Chart: UDISE+ playground-availability rate by school type/state.
  • Map: none essential.
  • Visual: a school playground or urban public-space scene, contrast pair (well-resourced vs. under-resourced).
  • Sources: UDISE+ 2023–24, RTE Act infrastructure norms, MoSPI Time Use Survey.
