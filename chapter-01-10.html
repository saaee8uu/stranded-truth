CHAPTER RESEARCH EDITION

The argument, evidence and visual route

This chapter is expanded through the research plan below. It is written as a reading route, not a placeholder checklist.

  • Narrative angle: closing chapter of Part I — synthesize the prior 9 threads into a single composite "starting conditions" profile, explicitly showing how birthplace + wealth + caste + language + health + documents + geography compound rather than act independently.
  • Historical context: brief recap linking each prior chapter's institutional history into one timeline.
  • Mechanism: explain compounding/interaction effects (not additive) — e.g., a low-wealth rural household in a weak-service state faces multiplicatively higher risk than any single factor alone predicts.
  • India context: intergenerational mobility research (Asher/Novosad/Rafkin) is the natural anchor for this synthesis chapter, since it's designed to measure exactly this compounding.
  • Examples: a composite "starting conditions index" thought experiment (explicitly labeled illustrative, not a real published index, unless the integrator finds a genuine published composite index to cite instead).
  • Counterarguments: individual exceptions and mobility are real and important to state clearly here, since this synthesis chapter is at highest risk of reading deterministically if not carefully hedged.
  • Limitations: no single study measures all 9 factors jointly for India; synthesis necessarily draws on separate studies with different samples/periods.
  • Data needed [VERIFY]: Asher/Novosad/Rafkin (2024) headline mobility numbers, reused from earlier chapters for a closing composite view.
  • Chart: a summary "which starting factors matter most" chart if a defensible source exists; otherwise a schematic (clearly labeled interpretive, per the project's own visual rule) showing the interaction, not a real data chart.
  • Map: none needed; this is a synthesis chapter.
  • Visual: none needed, or a symbolic transition image (e.g., a school gate, bridging into Part II/III).
  • Sources: all Part I sources, cross-referenced; Asher/Novosad/Rafkin as anchor.
