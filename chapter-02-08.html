THE QUESTION

What happens when private life performs public work?

This chapter follows the family as an institution rather than a sentimental backdrop. It separates what can be measured from what must be interpreted, and it keeps exceptions visible.

01 · OPENING SCENE

The ordinary room where systems become personal

A household decision can look private while carrying the pressure of wages, housing, law, caste, gender, schooling and health. The chapter begins at that junction: not with a slogan, but with the decisions people must make under unequal constraints.

The aim is not to portray every family as oppressive or every tradition as fixed. It is to ask which choices are genuinely available, who pays for care, who can leave, and which advantages survive from one generation to the next.

02 · HISTORY

Institutions do not begin at the front door

Kinship, property rules, marriage practices, welfare policy, migration and labour markets have repeatedly changed the work expected from families. Historical sections will distinguish legal change from social persistence and national patterns from regional variation.

03 · EVIDENCE BOARD

What must be measured

Elder carePensionsMigrationHealth access

The evidence trail combines household surveys, time-use data, health and education records, law and court material, demographic indicators, peer-reviewed research and documentary reporting. No single source is allowed to stand in for the whole institution.

04 · MECHANISM

Dependence, bargaining power and unequal exit

Outcomes are shaped by who controls income and assets, who performs unpaid work, who has mobility, whose reputation is considered fragile, and whether public systems provide an alternative to family dependence.

05 · COUNTER-EVIDENCE

Where the argument can fail

Families also protect people from market shocks, illness, unemployment and weak public services. Aggregate patterns can conceal supportive households, changing norms and regional exceptions. The chapter therefore treats contradiction as evidence, not inconvenience.
